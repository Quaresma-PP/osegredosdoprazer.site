Who was Saint Padre Pio?

Life and History

Saint Padre Pio of Pietrelcina (1887-1968) was an Italian Capuchin friar known for his profound life of prayer, the gift of the stigmata (the wounds of Christ on his own body), and for countless reports of graces obtained through his intercession.

The Gift of the Stigmata

For 50 years, he bore the wounds of Christ on his own body—one of the few saints with this phenomenon documented by science and the Church. These wounds remained open and bleeding, but without infection, something inexplicable to medicine.

Devotion to the Guardian Angel

From childhood, he saw and conversed with his guardian angel. He would often send his guardian angel to console and protect those in need, and advise his spiritual children to do the same. This special ability profoundly shaped his spirituality.

How comforting it is to know that near us there is a spirit who, from the cradle to the grave, never leaves us, not even when we dare to sin!

— Saint Padre Pio
