DAY 5

Prayer for Inner Peace

"Peace I leave with you; my peace I give you. I do not give to you as the world gives" (John 14:27).

— Words of Jesus that NOSSA SENHORA lived intensely

Reflection

We arrive at the fifth day, dedicated to crying out for that gift which surpasses all understanding: inner peace. Amidst daily tribulations, worries about the future, painful memories of the past, and anxieties of the present, it is very easy to lose the serenity of the soul.

The peace that Christ offers us is born from trust in God and the certainty of being loved by Him, even in the midst of storms. It is a peace that can coexist with challenges—NOSSA SENHORA, for example, faced countless persecutions, physical suffering, and spiritual battles, yet in his heart reigned the trust and surrender that bring peace.

To seek inner peace, it is essential to forgive and ask for forgiveness, to release the chains of guilt through reconciliation, and to nourish the soul with prayer. NOSSA SENHORA considered prayer as the "oxygen of the soul"—without it, our inner self drowns in anguish; with it, we breathe in divine hope and consolation.

Full Prayer

"Lord Jesus, Prince of Peace, my heart is in search of serenity. You know well how the winds of worry have stirred the waters of my soul. Therefore, just as the apostles cried out in the midst of the storm, I cry out to You today: Lord, save me, I am perishing! Come and calm the waves of my thoughts, come and rebuke the winds of my anxiety, saying: 'Peace! Be still!' so that a great calm may return to my inner being.

Pour out, O Jesus, Your peace that the world cannot give. This peace that does not depend on the absence of problems, but is the fruit of the certainty that You are with me at all times. Increase my faith so that I may firmly believe that nothing happens without Your permission, and that, if You permit it, even crosses can become a path to sanctification and a greater good. Cast away from me the spirit of fear and confusion that the evil one tries to sow. Like Nossa Senhora Desatadora de Nós, I want to always repeat: 'Jesus, I trust in You!' and thus rest confidently in Your Heart.

Forgive me, Lord, for the sins that steal my peace. I recognize that much of my restlessness is born when I stray from You or when I hold grudges against my brothers and sisters. Give me the grace of sincere forgiveness: that I may know how to forgive those who have offended me (for I do not want to carry the peace-stealing weight of resentment) and that I may also humbly ask for forgiveness from those I have offended. Break, Lord, all pride in me and heal wounded relationships. Reconcile me to You through true repentance and reconcile me with others through love. Thus, by eliminating these obstacles, peace will flow freely in my soul once more.

Holy Spirit, Comforter of souls, fill me with Your presence. Where Your Spirit is, there is freedom, serenity, and trust. Cradle my restless heart as a mother cradles a crying baby. Make me feel truly a beloved child of God, held in Your arms. In Your divine silence, speak to my heart, reinforcing that I am loved, that I have value in Your eyes, and that no evil shall separate me from Your love. May this certainty bring calm to my feelings and thoughts.

Lord, teach me to rest in You. May I seek moments of silence and prayer daily to converse with You and to breathe spiritually. I remember the example of NOSSA SENHORA: even amidst so much suffering, he found peace in long hours before the Tabernacle, surrendering everything to You. Perhaps I cannot be physically before You in the Tabernacle at all times, but I want now to open the tabernacle of my heart and welcome You within me.

Stay with me, Lord, and I will fear nothing, for You are my Shepherd, and I shall not want. I thank You, in advance, for the peace that is beginning to flood my being. I feel that you can say to me as you said to the forgiven sinner: 'Go in peace, your faith has saved you.' Yes, Lord, my faith is in You, and from You I receive peace. Remain with me so that this peace may be lasting and that I may also bring it to others around me. May my family, my friends, and all with whom I interact perceive the change and also feel this peace that comes from You. Thank you, Jesus, for giving me Your Peace. I receive it with all my heart. Amen."
